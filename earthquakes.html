Earthquakes

Prepare for Earthquake

1. General Earthquake Information:

  • Earthquakes Overview:
    • Earthquakes are sudden and rapid shaking of the ground caused by the shifting of tectonic plates beneath the Earth's surface.
    • They can cause significant damage to buildings, roads, and infrastructure and may trigger secondary disasters like tsunamis and landslides.
    • Earthquakes can strike without warning and vary greatly in intensity and duration.

2. Before an Earthquake:

  • Know Your Risk:
    • Identify if you live in an earthquake-prone area by checking seismic hazard maps.
    • Sign up for local earthquake alerts and warning systems.
  • Prepare Your Home:
    • Secure heavy furniture and appliances to walls to prevent them from toppling.
    • Store breakable and heavy items on lower shelves.
    • Identify safe spots in each room, such as under sturdy furniture, where you can take cover during an earthquake.
    • Create a family emergency plan and practice "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" drills.
    • Assemble an emergency kit with essentials like water, non-perishable food, medications, and first aid supplies.
  • Insurance:
    • Consider purchasing earthquake insurance, as standard homeowner’s insurance typically doesn’t cover earthquake damage.

3. During an Earthquake:

  • If Indoors:
    • Drop to your hands and knees to prevent being knocked over.
    • Cover your head and neck to protect yourself from falling debris.
    • Hold on to a sturdy piece of furniture until the shaking stops.
  • If Outdoors:
    • Move away from buildings, streetlights, and utility wires.
    • Once in an open area, drop to the ground and stay there until the shaking stops.
  • If in a Vehicle:
    • Stop the vehicle as quickly as possible, avoiding stopping under overpasses, bridges, or near buildings.
    • Stay in the vehicle until the shaking stops.

4. After an Earthquake:

  • Safety Checks:
    • Check yourself and others for injuries and administer first aid if needed.
    • Be prepared for aftershocks, which are smaller tremors that follow the main earthquake.
  • Home Safety:
    • Inspect your home for structural damage, gas leaks, and water leaks. If you suspect any danger, evacuate and seek professional help.
    • Avoid using open flames, as there may be gas leaks.
  • Communication:
    • Use text messages or social media to communicate with family and friends, as phone lines may be congested.
    • Listen to local authorities and follow their instructions regarding evacuation or return to your home.
  • Community Help:
  • Assist neighbors, especially those who may need help, such as the elderly or disabled.
  • Participate in community recovery efforts and be aware of shelters and resources available in your area.
