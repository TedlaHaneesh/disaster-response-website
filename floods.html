Floods

Prepare for Floods

1. General Flood Information:

Floods Overview:

  • Flooding is a temporary overflow of water onto normally dry land.
  • It is the most common disaster in the U.S. and can result from various factors, including rain, snow, coastal storms, and dam overflows.
  • Floods can cause outages, disrupt transportation, damage buildings, and trigger landslides.

2. Before a Flood:

Know Your Risk:

  • Visit FEMA's Flood Map Service Center to identify flood risks in your area.
  • Sign up for community warning systems and emergency alerts.

Purchase Flood Insurance:

  • Homeownerâ€™s insurance policies typically do not cover flood damage.
  • Purchase or renew flood insurance well before a disaster occurs, as policies usually take 30 days to become effective.

Prepare Your Home:

  • Make an evacuation plan, including for pets, and practice evacuation routes and shelter plans.
  • Gather essential supplies like non-perishable food, water, and cleaning supplies for several days.
  • Protect important documents in waterproof containers and create digital copies.
  • Move valuables to higher levels and declutter drains and gutters.
  • Install check valves and consider using a sump pump with a battery backup.

3. During a Flood:

Evacuation:

  • Evacuate immediately if instructed, and never drive around barricades.
  • Avoid walking, swimming, or driving through floodwaters.

Safety Tips:

  • Stay off bridges over fast-moving water.
  • If trapped in a car or building by rising water, move to the highest level or get on the roof and signal for help.
  • Listen to emergency alerts for updated information and instructions.

4. After a Flood:

Return Safely:

  • Return home only when authorities declare it safe.
  • Avoid driving except in emergencies.

Cleanup Precautions:

  • Wear heavy gloves, protective clothing, and masks during cleanup.
  • Avoid entering buildings with visible mold or water leaks if you have respiratory conditions.
  • Be cautious of animals, electrocution risks, and contaminated floodwater.

Generator Safety:

  • Use generators and gasoline-powered machinery only outdoors and away from windows to prevent carbon monoxide poisoning.
