Wildfires

Prepare for Wildfires

1. General Wildfire Information:

Wildfires Overview:

  • Wildfires are unplanned fires that burn in natural areas like forests, grasslands, or prairies.
  • These fires spread quickly and can devastate wildlife, natural areas, and communities.

2. Before a Wildfire:

Recognize Warnings and Alerts:

  • Have multiple ways to receive alerts, such as the FEMA app and local community alerts.
  • Be aware of the Emergency Alert System (EAS) and Wireless Emergency Alert (WEA).
  • Pay attention to air quality alerts.

Make an Emergency Plan:

  • Ensure everyone in your household knows what to do if you need to quickly evacuate.
  • Include plans for your workplace, kids' daycare, and other frequently visited places.

Review Important Documents:

  • Ensure insurance policies and personal documents, like IDs, are up to date.
  • Make copies and store them in a secure, password-protected digital space.

Strengthen Your Home:

  • Use fire-resistant materials for building, renovating, or repairs.
  • Create a fire-resistant zone around your home, free of leaves, debris, or flammable materials, at least 30 feet away.
  • Set up a portable air cleaner in a room that can be closed off from outside air during smoky conditions.

Know Your Evacuation Zone:

  • Learn your evacuation routes and practice with household members and pets.
  • Follow instructions from local authorities for the latest safety measures.

Gather Supplies:

  • Prepare enough supplies for your household, including a first aid kit, in your go-bag or car trunk.
  • Store an N95 mask to protect yourself from smoke inhalation.
  • Keep your cell phone charged and have backup charging devices ready.

3. During a Wildfire:

Stay Safe:

  • Pay attention to emergency alerts and evacuate immediately if instructed by authorities.
  • Check for open public shelters using the FEMA or Red Cross apps.
  • If trapped, call 9-1-1, provide your location, and turn on lights to help rescuers find you.
  • Use an N95 mask or limit exposure to smoke by staying indoors in a safe location.

4. After a Wildfire:

Return Safely:

  • Do not return home until authorities declare it safe.
  • Avoid hot ash, charred trees, smoldering debris, and live embers.

Cleanup Precautions:

  • Wear protective clothing and use a respirator during cleanup efforts.
  • Document property damage with photographs and contact your insurance company for assistance.
  • Use text messages or social media to communicate with family and friends.

Additional Resources

  • National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA): Provides real-time alerts and safety tips.
  • National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) : The force is trained to respond to natural and man-made disasters, and they are deployed across the country to assist in rescue and relief operations.
  • Local Emergency Services: List contact information for local emergency management agencies and shelters.

    • Printable Checklists: downloadable and printable checklists for emergency kits and evacuation plans.
    • Guides for Specific Needs: Provide guides tailored to specific needs, such as seniors, people with disabilities.
